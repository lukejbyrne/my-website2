From Enterprise Consulting to Full-Time Creator

In December 2025 I handed in my notice at a comfortable consulting job and went all-in on Byrne Media. People thought I was mad. Some days, I agreed with them.

The Corporate Years

I spent years at Accenture and Amdocs shipping Azure migrations for telecoms clients. It was good work,I learned to operate at scale, manage stakeholders, and deliver under pressure. But the gap between what I was capable of building and what I was allowed to build kept growing.

On evenings and weekends I was making YouTube videos about AI tools, and the channel was growing faster than my career. Eventually the maths stopped making sense. The side project had more upside than the day job.

The First Six Months

Going full-time as a creator is a crash course in doing everything yourself. Content, editing, sponsorship deals, product development, accounting, community management,it all lands on you. The freedom is real, but so is the cognitive load.

What saved me was treating it like a business from day one. Byrne Media Ltd was already set up. I had revenue from multiple streams,AdSense, sponsorships, Skool memberships, affiliate deals. It wasn't a leap of faith; it was a calculated transition.

What I'd Tell Someone Considering It

Don't quit your job to "find yourself." Quit your job because the alternative is already working and you need more hours in the day. Have revenue before you have freedom. Build the plane while you're still on the runway, not after you've jumped.

The biggest surprise? I don't miss the stability. I miss some of the people, but not the structure. When you're building something of your own, the uncertainty feels different. It's fuel, not fear.

What's Next

The plan is simple: grow the YouTube channel, ship more products, and build the community. Every month the revenue gets more diversified and the flywheel spins a little faster. The goal isn't to replace my old salary,it's to build something that would have been impossible inside a corporation.
