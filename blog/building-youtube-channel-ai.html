Building a YouTube Channel Around AI Tools

When I started making videos about AI tools, the niche barely existed. Now it's one of the fastest-growing categories on YouTube. Here's what I've learned growing Build with Luke to over 40,000 subscribers.

The Two-Tier Strategy

My audience is roughly 63% beginners. That shapes everything. I run two types of content in parallel: feature videos for growth and tutorials for conversion. A video like "Claude Code vs Antigravity" pulls in new viewers. A walkthrough on building a SaaS with Claude Code converts them into community members.

The feature videos get the views. The tutorials build the trust. You need both.

Formats That Work

After hundreds of videos, a few formats consistently outperform:

  • Live builds,real projects, real time, real mistakes. People love watching the process, not just the result.
  • Tool comparisons,"X vs Y" is catnip for YouTube's algorithm. Viewers search for these when they're making buying decisions.
  • Packaging refactors,taking an underperforming video and re-titling, re-thumbnailing, and re-describing it. This alone has doubled views on several videos.

Standing Out in a Crowded Niche

The AI tools space is getting crowded. What sets me apart is that I actually build things. I'm not reviewing tools from a press release,I'm shipping products with them and showing the receipts. Authenticity isn't a strategy; it's the only thing that scales.

I also lean into the builder identity. My community isn't passive consumers of AI news. They're people who want to make things. That filter keeps the content focused and the audience engaged.

The Business Model

YouTube ad revenue is a nice baseline, but it's not the business. The real model is: YouTube drives awareness, the free Skool community captures interest, and paid tiers plus SaaS products generate the majority of revenue. Sponsorships fill the gaps,I charge $600 for a dedicated video and $300 for a mid-roll, always demo-first, never scripted by the brand.

If you're thinking about starting a channel in this space, my advice is simple: pick a tool, build something real with it, and hit record. The audience for builders is growing faster than the supply of genuine builders making content.
